Welcome to our dedicated website to the father of Unix and C


Do you know that Dennis Ritchie worked in the Computing Sciences Research Center of Bell Labs until 2005? After that, a reorganization redistributed this group and named Convergence, Software, and Computer Science Laboratory. Click on the image to take a look at the Bell Labs website!

Do you know that Unix development started in 1969? It was at Bell Labs, by the hand of Dennis Ritchie, Ken Thompson, Brian Kernighan, Douglas McIlroy, and Joe Ossanna. It was designed to be small and adjustable for the programmers needs. It was the beginning of something different that later would build the base of what computers are today.

Do you know that Unix was written in C programming language? Dennis Ritchie created C with the intention of being implemented in operating systems. It was proved that it could be used for several applications, being nowadays independent of Unix and one of the most powerful high-level programming languages.



"My work was fairly theoretical. It was in recursive function theory. And in particular, hierarchies of functions in terms of computational complexity. I got involved in real computers and programming mainly by being - well, I was interested even as I came to graduate school."

Dennis Ritchie
